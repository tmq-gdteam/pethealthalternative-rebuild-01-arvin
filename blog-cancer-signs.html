10 Early Signs of Cancer in Pets

By Dr. Cheryl A. Caputo Â· Pet Health Alternatives

Just like in people, many pet cancers are treatable when they're caught early. Learning the warning signs - and acting quickly when you spot one - gives your pet the best possible chance. Below are ten early signs every pet owner, especially dog owners, should watch for. If you notice any of them, contact your veterinarian as soon as possible.

A cancer diagnosis is every pet owner's nightmare, but early detection changes outcomes. Educating yourself about the warning signs and keeping your pet healthy are two of the most powerful things you can do.

The 10 early warning signs

  1. Lumps or bumps. The most common way to detect cancer is finding a lump on or under the skin. Make it a habit to check your pet's skin - especially around the face and behind the ears.
  2. Unusual odors. An offensive smell from the mouth, nose, or rectal area can signal a tumor. Don't ignore it.
  3. Sudden weight loss. If your pet is on a regular diet but loses weight unexpectedly, it may point to a serious underlying illness.
  4. Loss of appetite. A sudden lack of interest in meals and favorite treats can be a sign of a health condition, including cancer.
  5. Wounds that won't heal. Sores that don't heal should be checked promptly, as they can indicate a more serious medical condition.
  6. Abnormal bleeding or discharge. Unexplained bleeding or discharge can be an early sign of cancer or an infection.
  7. Digestive trouble. Frequent diarrhea or vomiting, along with bloating or swelling around the abdomen, warrants a veterinary visit.
  8. Sudden lameness or low energy. Unexplained limping or a drop in energy can be caused by serious illness. If your pet isn't acting normal, call your vet.
  9. Breathing problems. Shortness of breath with coughing or sneezing after brief activity - walking, running, or playing - could be an early sign of lung cancer.
  10. Irritability. If your normally easygoing pet becomes irritable or snappy, it may be reacting to pain. Watch closely for other symptoms.
