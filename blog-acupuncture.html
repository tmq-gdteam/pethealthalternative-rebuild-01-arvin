Acupuncture

What Can Veterinary Acupuncture Do for Your Dog or Cat?

By Dr. Cheryl A. Caputo Â· Pet Health Alternatives

Veterinary acupuncture can relieve pain, ease gastrointestinal and skin problems, calm respiratory issues, and support healing for both dogs and cats. By stimulating specific points on the body, it improves blood flow, activates the nervous system, and prompts the release of the body's own anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving hormones - often alongside conventional care.

Integrative treatment methods are gaining popularity in the pet world, and acupuncture is one of the most well established. Practiced for hundreds of years, it's seen by many pet parents as a gentle way to relieve their dog's or cat's pain - and it may even help with certain illnesses.

What makes veterinary acupuncture effective?

Acupuncture aims to stimulate the body to restore itself. It promotes healing by correcting energy imbalances, improving blood flow, stimulating the nervous system, and boosting the release of anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving hormones.

It works by inserting thin needles into body tissue where nerve bundles and blood vessels meet. These points - known as acupuncture points - sit along meridians, or energy channels, that run across the body's surface. The meridians allow a cycle of energy to travel throughout the whole body within 24 hours.

Other forms of acupuncture

Electroacupuncture

Electroacupuncture attaches the needles to a device that generates tiny electrical pulses to stimulate the acupuncture points, producing a stronger form of stimulation. It's often used for musculoskeletal disorders including back pain, muscle soreness, chronic arthritis, hip dysplasia, slipped disk, degenerative joint disease, and other conditions of the nervous system.

LED (light-emitting diode)

LED therapy uses a silicon microchip that produces different light colors when stimulated by electrical currents. Unlike lasers, which pinpoint a narrow beam, LEDs spread the light out over a wider surface area - allowing easier application and faster treatment. It's a great option for needle-sensitive pets.

Aquapuncture

With aquapuncture, a solution - usually saline combined with vitamin B12 - is injected directly into the acupuncture points using a hypodermic needle. The solution continues to stimulate those points for several days. Aquapuncture is ideal for dogs suffering from severe hip or back pain caused by arthritis, vertebral spondylosis, or hip dysplasia.

How does acupuncture help my dog or cat?

  1. Pain management. One of the most common uses, often paired with conventional treatment. Stressful procedures like chemotherapy are frequently combined with acupuncture to help pets feel better.
  2. Musculoskeletal problems. Conditions like hip dysplasia, nerve injuries, and arthritis can improve with acupuncture, and it's also useful for rehabbing a physical injury.
  3. Skin problems. Granulomas, allergic reactions, dermatitis, and hot spots can respond well. Improved circulation aids healing and helps reduce itchiness.
  4. Gastrointestinal issues. Acupuncture can ease diarrhea and nausea and help stabilize digestion by stimulating the digestive process.
  5. Respiratory problems. Pets with asthma and allergies often benefit from acupuncture's anti-inflammatory and calming effects.

Acupuncture and other alternative healing methods can meaningfully improve your pet's quality of life. Wondering whether your dog or cat should try it?
